VirtuGhan
virtughan.live

My Work:

Worked on ideation and implementation together with my friend Kshitij. Developed the mapping functionalities and frontend. This project was done with self interest after exposed to the Sen2Cube data cube while studying at the University of Salzburg.


About the Project:

Virtughan is a initiative that emphasizes efficient on-the-fly tile computation for satellite imagery using open-source tools. By leveraging Cloud Optimized GeoTIFFs (COGs) with Sentinel-2 imagery, it enables real-time calculations at various zoom levels without the need for pre-processing or extensive storage. This approach highlights how scalable and efficient computation can be achieved even with limited hardware resources. Additionally, Virtughan introduces the concept of virtual data cubes, where the focus is on computation rather than storage. By stacking and processing images over time, data cube systems can perform analyses across multiple layers without duplicating original data. The integration of COGs, the SpatioTemporal Asset Catalog (STAC) API, and NumPy arrays allows for improved access and processing of large Earth observation datasets. This method ensures optimal resolution, fast performance, and minimal server overhead, making it easier to handle and analyze extensive datasets quickly and efficiently.

Nepal, located in a seismically active zone, is highly prone to earthquakes due to the ongoing collision between the Indian Plate and the Eurasian Plate. Agent-Based Modeling for Rainfall-Runoff Simulation
click here to view more details

My Work:

This project was done together with classmate Rabina as the part of course study Spatial Simulation at the University of Salzburg.


About the Project:

Nepal experienced increased rainfall in recent years, leading to floods even in traditionally low-precipitation areas. To understand and predict these floods, this project focused on the Bagmati River, a major cause of urban flooding in Kathmandu during monsoons. By developing and validating a runoff model using precipitation sensor data and river water level measurements, the research aimed to accurately simulate hydrological dynamics. The model, built on the GAMA platform, was tested against observed data and various rainfall scenarios. Despite limitations like low-resolution data and simplified assumptions, this study aimed to improve our understanding of flood dynamics and predictive capabilities through an agent-based modeling approach.

Geonadir is a global drone data repository. It helps drone pilots store, process, and share their drone mapping images, supporting scientists and environmental managers around the world to make data-driven decisions.

Overview of Product

DASTAA (Digital and Spatial Technologies for Anticipatory Action), developed by NAXA and IHRR, is an integrated digital platform designed to aid decision-making in anticipatory actions. This proactive approach identifies potential risks and adopts measures to mitigate their negative consequences before events occur, aiming to save lives and minimize losses.

About Product

DASTAA uses digital and spatial technology to anticipate household risks and send tailored early action messages. It addresses the lack of data-driven disaster preparedness in vulnerable communities, particularly in low-income countries. The platform tackles issues like weak coordination, limited accessibility to risk communication, and the often reactive nature of disaster management activities.

  • Early Disaster Forecasting: Utilizes historical weather data and advanced analytics to predict disasters.
  • Early Warning System and Anticipatory Action: Provides real-time, location-specific early warnings and strategic interventions during imminent disasters, including IVR alerts and pre-allocated relief funds.

Overview of Product

Drone Tasking Manager (DroneTM), developed by HOT and NAXA, is an open-source platform designed to democratize access to high-resolution aerial imagery. By leveraging community-driven collaboration and consumer-grade drones, DroneTM enables real-time, scalable, and cost-effective aerial mapping for disaster response, urban planning, and emergency management.

About Product

DroneTM eliminates the reliance on expensive satellite imagery or large drones, which are often unavailable in critical moments. Instead, it empowers individuals with small, affordable drones to collaboratively map large areas. The platform integrates flight planning, imagery collection, processing, and dissemination, allowing users to contribute to OpenAerialMap or keep data private as needed.
